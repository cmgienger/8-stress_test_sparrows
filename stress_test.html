Stress Test: Preparing for the Final

2024-12-02

We will be analyzing data from an experiment where sparrows were given supplemental food (via bird feeders) 1-5 times per month for the winter months of the year; birds were given either a high (100 g) or low (25 g) amount of supplemental food.

We are testing the hypothesis that supplemental feeding allows birds to have higher body mass in the spring. Some years had harsh winters and some years were normal.

First notice that the authors manipulated/quantified supplementation in two ways:

  • whether the supplement was hi (100g) or low (25g); a categorical measure

  • how often the supplementation was given; a continuous measure

Questions to work through with code, analyses, figures, models, etc.

  1. Do winter type and level of supplementation (feeding_group) work together to determine mass of sparrows? Present main and interaction effects.

  2. Are differences in Spring body mass of sparrows jointly determined by winter type and feeding frequency?

Questions to think about in preparing for final:

  1. What are the differences in how we specify the instructions for making these figures?

  2. What are the differences in how we model the data?

  3. Look at the structure of the data (i.e.whether variables are continuous, categorical, ordinal, etc.); what kind of analysis (statistical test) would you expect from the given data structure?

  4. Are there any variables you would expect to be converted to factors?

  5. What would you expect to be the main effects for this analysis? How about covariates?

  6. Would you expect any interactions among variables?

  7. How would you visually determine whether a significant interaction is expected?

  8. How do you model an interaction effect? (how is it specified in code)

  9. What do significant interactions among main effects tell us?

  10. What do non-significant interactions tell us?

  11. If there are no significant interaction effects among variables, we can then proceed directly to comparing the levels of main effects and all possible combinations of variables to determine which is best, second-best, worst, etc.

  12. If there are significant interaction effects among variables, we can only compare the possible combinations of variables (cannot make much inference for main effects).

  13. Are there pairwise differences among levels of the main effect variables (i.e.are they different at their adjusted [marginal] means)?
